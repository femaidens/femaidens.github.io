Connecting to the Limelight

There are two ways to connect to the limelight:

    1. limelight.local.5801
    2. Using the Limelight Finder app

If the computer is connected to the radio and limelight.local:5801 doesn’t work, then you have to use the Limelight Finder app to find the limelight.

To use the Limelight Finder app:

  1. Open the Limelight Finder.
  2. Click Find Limelights.
  3. Once you see our limelight (the team # should say 2265), click on it and then click Configure Selected.
  4. This should open a local host on your computer!

Configuring the Pipeline

  • Wire and connect limelight to radio and RoboRio
  • Press red button (off) + switch (on) connected to battery to turn on and off
  • Connect laptop to radio through wifi network (ex: 2265_Kermit). The laptop needs to be connected to the radio in order to connect to the limelight.
  • Go to website: limelight.local:5801

Aligning the Limelight

  • Goal: The red box should align with the edges of the retroreflective tape and the blue box should align to match the tape as well (example and extra tips further down).
  • Make the retroreflective tape as distinctive from the background as possible.
  • Use the red lines to guide you. Notice the location of the pixels -- make sure the tape is completely outlined with red pixels.
  1. Input → Change Exposure first → Move onto editing Black Level Offset, Red Balance, and Blue Balance(the lower the red balance, the less red there is, the lower the blue balance, the less blue there is)
  2. Thresholding → Eyedropper on one corner of retroreflective tape → Include Pixel on one of the other corners/edges
  3. Play around with the values under Thresholding (start from big to small): Make sure Thresholding bars don't get too small
  4. Contour Filtering: Play around with W/H Ratio (yellow rect) and Smart Speckle Rejection
  5. Keep messing with the Input and Thresholding values until all of the outlines are steady.
  6. As a final check, move the camera and board around to check that the outlines are stable. The blue box should be stable no matter how the target or limelight moves.

Tips

  • Use Include Pixel to steady the blue and yellow box.
  • Never use Ignore Pixel.
  • Don’t turn the Black Level Offset to 0.
  • Don’t let Hue or Value get too small.
  • Use the arrow keys to move the bar by 1.
  • Make the bar for the values under Thresholding large at first and continue to decrease it to get the right value (go from big to small).

Terms: (defined more clearly in the powerpoint linked above)

  • Change exposure: changes the brightness, helps with having the reflective tape stand out from the background
  • Black Level Offset: changes “blackness”/darkness of background. Do not turn all the way down.
